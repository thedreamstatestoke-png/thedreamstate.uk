Privacy Policy

The Dream State Limited · Company No. 16801178 · UK GDPR & DPA 2018 · Last updated: 2026

Working draft. This Privacy Policy is a clear, good-faith starting point for launch and will be replaced by a solicitor-reviewed version. It explains, in plain terms, what data we collect and how we handle it.
1 · Controller2 · What we collect3 · Why4 · Lawful basis5 · Sharing6 · Retention7 · Your rights8 · Cookies

1. Who controls your data

The data controller is The Dream State Limited (Company No. 16801178), Burslem, Stoke-on-Trent, England. For any privacy question or request, contact thedreamstate.sje@gmail.com.

2. What we collect

  • Information you give us — your name, email address, business name, website, and the details you share when you book a call, complete the diagnostic, send the contact form, or engage us (such as rough revenue and ad-spend ranges).
  • Information collected automatically — basic technical and usage data such as IP address, device and browser type, and pages viewed, where analytics are enabled.
  • Client project data — materials and access you provide so we can deliver an engagement.

3. Why we use it

To respond to enquiries and book calls; to provide, manage and improve our services; to send service-related communications; to keep records and meet legal and accounting obligations; and, where you have agreed, to send occasional marketing you can opt out of at any time.

4. Lawful basis

We rely on: contract (to provide services you request), legitimate interests (to run and grow our business in a way that respects your rights), consent (for optional marketing and non-essential cookies), and legal obligation (for tax and accounting records).

5. Who we share it with

We do not sell your personal data. We share it only with trusted service providers who help us operate — for example email, scheduling, payment, hosting and analytics providers — and only as needed to deliver our service, and with authorities where legally required. Some providers may process data outside the UK; where they do, we take steps to ensure an appropriate level of protection.

6. How long we keep it

We keep personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes above, including any period required to meet legal, accounting or reporting requirements. Enquiry data we no longer need is deleted or anonymised.

7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to access, correct, delete or restrict the use of your personal data, to object to certain processing, to data portability, and to withdraw consent at any time. To exercise any of these, email us. You also have the right to complain to the UK's Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

8. Cookies

This site uses only the cookies necessary to function and, where enabled and with your agreement, basic analytics to understand how the site is used. You can control or block cookies through your browser settings. We will update this section if additional cookies are introduced.
